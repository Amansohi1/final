About Us

Amazon.com, Inc. AMAZON is an American multinational technology company focusing on e-commerce, cloud computing, online advertising, digital streaming, and artificial intelligence. It has been often referred to as "one of the most influential economic and cultural forces in the world,"[5] and is often regarded as one of the world's most valuable brands.[6] It is considered to be one of the Big Five American technology companies, alongside Alphabet (parent company of Google), Apple, Meta (formerly Facebook, Inc.) and Microsoft. is one of the world's most valuable companies and, as of 2023, is the world's most valuable automaker. In 2022, the company led the battery electric vehicle market, with 18% share. Amazon was founded by Jeff Bezos from his garage in Bellevue, Washington,[7] on July 5, 1994. E-Commerce Giant

Amazon stands as a towering e-commerce giant, fundamentally reshaping the landscape of online shopping. Its expansive digital marketplace offers an unparalleled selection of products, spanning from everyday essentials to niche items, all conveniently accessible at the click of a button. With a customer-centric approach, Amazon's user-friendly platform and efficient delivery systems have not only streamlined shopping experiences but have also set new industry standards for customer satisfaction and convenience.

Prime Video

Central to Amazon's multifaceted success is its innovative Prime subscription service. Beyond its hallmark expedited shipping benefits, Prime encompasses a comprehensive ecosystem that enriches subscribers' lives. A noteworthy facet of this ecosystem is Prime Video, Amazon's distinctive streaming platform. Prime Video has emerged as a major contender in the entertainment industry, boasting a diverse library of original content, blockbuster movies, and binge-worthy TV shows. From captivating dramas to uproarious comedies.

Technological Advancements

Renowned for its trailblazing spirit, Amazon consistently drives innovation and harnesses technology to redefine conventional norms. The introduction of Amazon Go, a revolutionary concept of cashier-less stores, exemplifies the company's commitment to frictionless shopping experiences. Moreover, Amazon Web Services (AWS), the company's groundbreaking cloud computing platform, has catalyzed the digital transformation of businesses worldwide. By pushing the boundaries of technological possibilities, Amazon has not only revolutionized its operations but has also inspired new paradigms across diverse sectors.

Disruptor of Markets

Beyond its commercial endeavors, Amazon has been an unparalleled disruptor, reshaping industries and consumer behaviors alike. From reimagining the book market with Kindle and challenging the traditional grocery shopping model with Amazon Fresh, the company has consistently shattered conventional norms. This far-reaching impact extends to cultural facets, as Amazon's prevalence has influenced modern consumer behaviors, from how people shop to how they read and consume media. Amazon's ubiquitous presence has made it a cultural emblem of the digital era, reflecting a transformative force that continues to shape the world as we know it.

Jeffrey Preston Bezos

Founder CEO

Jeffrey Preston Bezos born January 12, 1964 is an American business magnate, media proprietor, and investor. He is the founder, executive chairman, and former president and CEO of Amazon, the world's largest e-commerce and cloud computing company. With a net worth of US$150 billion as of July 2023, Bezos is the third-wealthiest person in the world and was the wealthiest from 2017 to 2021, according to both the Bloomberg Billionaires Index and Forbes.

The first centibillionaire on the Forbes Real Time Billionaires Index and the second ever to have eclipsed the feat since Bill Gates in 1999,[6] Bezos was named the "richest man in modern history" after his net worth increased to $150 billion in July 2018.[7] In August 2020, according to Forbes, he had a net worth exceeding $200 billion.[8] In 2020 during the COVID-19 pandemic, his wealth grew by approximately $24 billion.[9] On July 5, 2021, Bezos stepped down as the CEO and president of Amazon and took over the role of executive chairman. AWS CEO Andy Jassy succeeded Bezos as the CEO and president of Amazon. In September 2022, he was ranked second on the Forbes 400 list of wealthiest Americans, with a net worth of $151 billion.

Andrew R. Jassy

President

Andrew R. Jassy (born January 13, 1968) is an American business executive who is the president and chief executive officer (CEO) of Amazon. Before being appointed by Jeff Bezos and the Amazon board during the fourth quarter of 2020, Jassy served as the SVP and later as the CEO of Amazon Web Services from 2003 to 2021.

Jassy worked for five years after graduation before entering his MBA program as a project manager for a collectibles company, MBI, and then he and an MBI colleague started a company and closed it down. Jassy joined Amazon as a marketing manager in 1997 with several other Harvard MBA colleagues. In 2003, he and Jeff Bezos came up with the idea to create the cloud computing platform that became known as Amazon Web Services, which launched in 2006. Jassy headed it and its team of 57 people.
